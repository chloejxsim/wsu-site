WSU organises a number of competitions throughout the calendar year.

Debating

Regionals

The New Zealand School’s Debating Council (NZSDC) runs annual regional competitions to select teams for Nationals. Students of any ability from Years 9 to 13 are able to participate in the largest competition of Wellington’s debating calendar.

Impromptu Cup

The Wellington Speaking Union (WSU) runs the annual Impromptu Cup tournament for senior students. Students compete in teams of two with only 10 minutes preparation time.

British Parliamentary

NZSDC may elect to run a British Parliamentary tournament for senior students. Each debate hosts four competing teams of two who only receive 30 minutes preparation.

Junior Development

The annual Junior Development Competition (also known as “The Moot Must Fall Student Tournament”) is available to debaters of any ability from Years 9 to 11. Students are placed in mixed school teams, with a varied age and debating experience.

Public Speaking

Dunsheath Shield

The Dunsheath Shield is a public speaking competition organised and hosted by the WSU for junior students.

Stockley Cup

The Stockley Cup is a public speaking competition organised and hosted by the WSU for senior students.
